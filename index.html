What this is

A value tool for the World Cup knockout rounds. For each match it shows two independent models — our own Elo-based line and a third-party feed — side by side, converts our number to a fair (no-vig) price, and once you type in your book's prices returns the expected value of each side, the vig, and a ★ wherever both models beat the market on the same side. Two models plus the market agreeing is a far stronger flag than any one alone.

Our model: Elo → goals → prices

Built in two pieces, each from the source that's best for the job. Who's favoured comes from the World Football Elo gap, turned into a result expectancy (the eloratings winning-expectancy curve) and split into win/draw/win — so the result line rides the trusted strength rating, not a 3-game form blip. How many goals comes from each team's attack and defence (goals scored and conceded per game, regressed toward the mean for the short sample) — that sets the match total, which feeds a Poisson scoreline grid. From that one grid we read 1X2, Over/Under 2.5, both-teams-to-score, and the likely scorelines. So Elo fixes the side; the goal data fixes the total; everything else falls out consistently. The expected-goals line under each bar (e.g. 1.92–1.13) is what the model actually thinks will happen.

The feed, and the gap

The Feed column is a separate third-party match model — your cross-check, not gospel (treat it like the UFC finish-rate model). When our Elo line and the feed disagree by 8+ points a model-gap note appears: that's where to dig, because one of the two is wrong and the market may be sitting on the wrong one. Worth knowing why they can diverge: the feed appears to weight current-tournament form harder (it has Germany well over our Elo line), and it likely carries home-venue for the three hosts — which our model deliberately doesn't (see limits). On a USA/Mexico/Canada home match, trust the feed's lean more.

Honest limits of our model

The Matches tab is neutral site, so the hosts (USA, Mexico, Canada) are under-rated there on days they play at home — use the Matchup Lab's home toggle to add the bump (below). Attack/defence rest on 3–4 games and are noisy even after shrinking, so treat Over/Under and BTTS as directional, not precise; knockout games also trend lower-scoring than the group stage these totals were built on, so Overs are if anything generous. Independent Poisson slightly under-counts draws and 1-1/0-0, so the draw and BTTS-no fair prices are mildly conservative. The 1X2 line is 90 minutes; the separate to-advance line adds extra time and penalties (below).

To advance — extra time & penalties

A knockout that's level after 90 goes to 30 minutes of extra time, then a shootout. So advancing isn't the same as winning in 90, and the "To advance" line on each card prices the real market. Extra time is modelled as the same Poisson run at roughly a third of the pace (30 of 90 minutes); if it's still level, the shootout is treated as a coin flip. That last part is deliberate: penalty shootouts are empirically close to random and even good models barely beat 50%, so rather than invent a penalty rating we don't have, we call it 50/50 and flag it as the least certain part of the number. The upshot is useful — a heavy favourite's advance number sits well above its 90-minute win % (France 85% → ~93% to advance), while a slight favourite barely clears 50% because the draw mass resolves near-evenly (Mexico 36% → ~52%). When the to-advance price implies more certainty than that, there's an edge.

Matchup Lab & the home bump

The Matchup Lab runs the full model on any two teams in the field — look ahead to a bracket matchup that hasn't been drawn, price a hypothetical final, or settle a "who beats who". The home selector adds the standard +100-Elo home-field advantage (eloratings' convention) to one side, which is also the right way to handle the three hosts on home soil. It shows the same W/D/W, to-advance, totals, BTTS and likely scores as a real card, plus the ratings driving the result.

Stake sizer — EV & Kelly

In the Lab, pick any outcome and enter a price to get EV and the Kelly stake. Kelly is the bankroll fraction that maximises long-run growth: f = (p·d − 1)/(d − 1). Full Kelly is the theoretical optimum but swings hard and punishes any model error, so half-Kelly is the standard, sane default — and given this model isn't back-tested yet, even quarter-Kelly is defensible. A non-positive f means no edge: pass.

Title Odds — the bracket Monte Carlo

The real, locked knockout bracket (verified against FIFA's official match schedule and the third-place-team assignment table) is simulated 20,000 times. Each tie is decided by the model's to-advance probability — 90 minutes, then extra time, then the 50/50 shootout — and winners feed forward round by round to the final. Canada and Brazil have already won their Round-of-32 ties, so they're locked into the Round of 16; the other 14 R32 ties and all of the Round of 16, quarters, semis and final are simulated. The output is each team's chance to reach every round and to lift the trophy, with a no-vig fair price for the title — set that against your book's outrights and the gap is the futures edge. Two honest caveats: the four top seeds (Argentina, Spain, France, England) sit in four separate quarters, so the model's love of Elo gaps concentrates probability on them more than the market will — favourites read short here, longshots long. And six rounds of a neutral-site, no-injuries model compound, so deep-run numbers are a lens, not a lock.

Defence & possession columns

GA/90 (goals allowed per 90) and Poss% come straight from FBref's squad goalkeeping and standard tables — real, not modelled. Green GA/90 = stingy (≤0.70), red = leaky (≥2.0). Useful for knockout totals and clean-sheet reads, where Mexico and Spain (0.00) sit a world apart from Iraq and Tunisia (4.00).

Shot dominance & luck (the underlying lens)

Shot dom is the SoT ratio — your shots on target ÷ (yours + opponents'). Above 50% means you're creating more clear chances than you allow (Argentina 88%, Spain 84% lead). Luck (G−xGp) is goals scored-minus-conceded versus a transparent shot proxy (xGp = 0.30·SoT + 0.035·other shots): gold = banking more than the shots justify (riding finishing/variance — fade candidates), green = creating more than the scoreline shows (unlucky — back candidates). It's the same over/under-performance read you run on UFC finish rates.

Honest limit on the proxy

This is shot volume + on-target rate, not real xG — no shot locations, so a tap-in and a 30-yarder count the same on the off-target term. It's tournament aggregate over 3 games (small sample), and because it's aggregate, it does not feed back into the Power rating — Power is the World Football Elo and Shot dom / Luck sit beside it as a separate lens. Per-match xG would let a model fold them into a rating directly; FBref still isn't carrying it for this event.

The math

American → decimal: +a → 1+a/100, −a → 1+100/a. EV per $1 on an outcome with model probability p at decimal d: EV = p·d − 1. Fair price is 1/p. Vig on a 3-way is Σ(1/d) − 1. No-vig market probability is each 1/d rescaled to sum to 1 — that's the number to compare against the model.

Honest limits

This is a pre-match snapshot, not a pick. Our Elo→Poisson model is transparent but not back-tested here — no closing-line or Brier check yet, so treat it as decision support, not proof of edge (calibration logging is a planned upgrade). It's neutral-site and 90-minute only (see the model's own limits above). Live and just-finished matches are excluded from value. Shop your own books — the edge lives in beating the price shown — and method/round- specific soccer props carry heavy vig that a boost rarely overcomes, same lesson as the parlays.
